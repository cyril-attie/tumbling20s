Hello, Crisis!

A Crisis is a period in which prices of all assets fall.

It is often associated with a bad or wrong time for the economy, less for those who know what a crisis really is.

Indeed people who have their savings invested in some asset become frightened to have lost 50% of their savings. For most of the people it becomes a tough time. Clients have lost wealth and consume less so businesses struggle to earn revenue . Many employees get fired as companies fail to earn enough money to pay them. The blossoming unemployed people make the strive for revenue even more difficult. The falling revenues sweep a great portion of profits. The standard in the stock market is that the price of a company rises to 10 times the profits of the company. With a disappearing profits stock prices fall even more

However people that have been saving find a great opportunity to buy assets at a cheap price. Which is low risk investment.

Those who have lost their savings as well as those who become unemployed tend to think negatively. And that's how we can remember a crisis triggers an economic depression. An economic depression is a period of time after prices have fallen in which it is very difficult to start a business. This website is made for those who do not have an economic background to get a glance on how crises happen and how can we predict its arrival.
