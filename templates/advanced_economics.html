The T20 Model

The T20 model states monetary deflation and production surplus cause price deflation. And price deflation is the cause of falling revenue, profits and asset prices. Therefore indicators that warn of monetary deflation, like rising interest rates and debt being paid off, anticipate production surplus and crisis. For the curious: unless explicitly specified, "interest rates", in this paper, refers to the Effective Federal Funds interest rate which is the rate at which banks to borrow funds from each other overnight (for just one day). This rate leads all other interest rates and the central bank heavily influences this rate by trading in the short-term debt market.

Testing the T20 Model

The last two crises happened in spring 2000, named dot-com crisis, and in autumn 2008, named real estate crisis. At these times asset prices fell more than 50% in less than 3 years. Half of the wealth of people disapeared in panic.

The Dot-Com (.com) Crisis

Focusing on the dot-com crisis: between January 1999 and spring 2000 oil prices went from 10$/barrel to 35$/barrel. Oil is used to transport products and make packaging materials, so an increase in the price of oil causes price inflation. Resulting in interest rates climbing from 4.6% to 6.5% in roughly the same period. From spring 2000 to summer 2002, in two years, the Nasdaq 100, a sotck market index of tech companies fell from ~5000 to ~800. Near 85% of savings invested in these companies disapeared. Other indexes displayed also great losses:

  • SSE (China) from 2250 to 1000 ~55% loss
  • HSI (Hong-Kong) 18300 to 8300 ~55% loss

The Transition Between Crises

Interest rates remained under 2% for ~3 years (fall 2001 to fall 2004). The government heavily sponsored home-buyers and financed two wars, Afghanistan and Iraq. A massive quantity US dollars was poured into the economy to reflate prices and kick off consumerism. The monetary economy being reflated oil prices traded above 40$/barrel in 2004, which is a sing of inflation return. In the next two years the central bank rose rates again. From 1% to 5.25% in less than 2 years, that's a huge discouragement. Till then house prices had been rising thanks to the previous cheap credit, so they obviously stopped increasing. Rates stayed at 5.25% for more than a year. That was the indicator of a new crisis coming.

In effect, Bankers have injected $3,500 billion which is approximately 25% of the $14,000 billion existing as of 2019 in 6 years, from 2008 to 2014. A huge amount.

The below chart plots the central bank assets. Since 1945 until 2009 the central bank assets were stable, below 900 million dollars. Since 2008 to 2014 the central bank has acquired $3.5 trillion. Though originally the assets of the central bank were gold and silver that backed their liabilities that were central banks bills, now the game has radically changed. Since 2009 central banks store government and corporate bonds "as good as gold". But these government debts will be redeemed in terms of the central bank liability: the bill.

Basically to buy government bonds central banks just need to increase your bank account in some units. Inflating accounts like this results in rising prices, among them, the prices of the assets bought. When they retrieve money by selling the bonds in the market, prices will fall due to monetary deflation. That is a really assymetric game in which they decide the turning points of the market as they are the biggest player in the market. They have infinite money, as they write the bank accounts.

Stock of these central banks cannot be traded or pledged and, by law, earn 6% dividend on capital invested. This dividend shall be cumulative. Which means their share in the central bank can grow exponentially and then, the plan would be, to cash out future dividend when the equity share has grown. Compounding $1 at 6% for 100 years yields $339 for every dollar invested initially. Resulting in a dividend of ~$20 per $1 invested. That is for the US central bank that was the first modern central bank. In every country regulations regarding central bank profits distribution are different. However information asymetry is the same in every country. (The next paragraphs are a little technical)

Second, a very subtle greed indicator. In times of greed investors seek profit return, turning a blind eye on investment risk. This behavior results in a market event called inverted yield curve. In a normal yield curve, borrowing to long-term is more expensive yearly than borrowing to short-term and renovating the debt. This is 'normal' because statistically default risk increases the longer the duration of the loan. However when stock market prices are overvalued, when a crisis is imminent, smart investors (usually high wealth investors) get out of the stock market. And where do they put the money?

Before crises, smart money moves to government debt investment. In the context of a crisis, the central bank will lower interest rates to start reflating asset markets. Smart investors don't know how many years will the crisis and subsequent depression last, so they cautiously prefer to take long term bonds rather than short term ones. Short term bonds would have to be renovated soon at the lowered interest rates, earning less return. But by buying long term bonds smart money gets to receive a relatively high fixed income return for many years. A temporary refuge from the markets. Besides the old bonds (that earn a pre-crisis high interest rate) prices rise so that the yield (interest rate/price) balances with the new bonds earning post-crisis lower interest rate. So smart investors gain double: a safe stream of relatively high interest payments and bond price revaluation.

Serfdom vs Freedom

Economics deal with how individual efforts are integrated to satisfy needs to sustain life. But it can go further. For example, medicine not only deals with how the body works internally. But it goes further. Medicine deals with being healthy, with the diagnosis, treatment and prevention of diseases. The purpose of medicine is to save lives. Economist can go further too. They could deal with integrating efforts maximizing individual freedom and collective collaboration.

Actually success in life comes usually from the ability to integrate individual efforts in a solid collaboration. What successful solid collaboration integrate individuals? From couples to Governments, the financial system, religion and the company you work in. Even a freelancers are integrated directly to customers. Governement integrates individuals by preventing conflicts among them so each one can live as free as possible without bothering others. Finance is there to unite entrepreneurs with investors, so that the former can achieve their ideas and serve the sovereign consumers. Religion integrates people around stories, rituals and morality rules to help them connect with a long-term static purpose. Companies blend individual efforts to produce a demanded product or service. The common pattern to all collaborations is to unite people around a same purpose.

Purposes are like a seeds, and colletivities are the solid trees. All trees have the same shape, and the shape of collectivities is to unite people around a purpose. As Eric Raymond Steven and Clay Shirky have shown, once a purpose is defined collectivities can be organised top-down or bottom-up.

In top-down structures, called institutions, purpose(s) are decided by one (or some) that communicate to the many. Then the many, often blindly, execute. Corporations are created this way. The executive officer and the board of directors take decisions, decisions are communicated to managers and managers plan deadlines, organize departments, lead people and control execution is following the right course of action; and then report to executive directors so they take more decisions. In top-down structures people is directed. Top-down structures are opaque, rigidly ordered: we cannot easily speak with the decision maker and when we get to speak he/she often has a too busy or closed mind to listen to other initiatives. A wedding is a good example of top-down structure. Religion, government and the banking system are also pure top-down structures.

In bottom-up structures, called decentralized collaborations, small purposes that contribute to the collective purpose are set by all people. Bottom-up structures are usually born of the initiative of somebody. This creator starts implementing the idea and broadcasts to all people his implementation. As he works, other people having a similar purpose find interesting the project so they use it and contribute to it when they can. Communications are broadcasted to all the people. Eventually as anyone can see everything, the project is transparent and more secure. Also, people is more motivated as they have a personal interest that contribute to achieve the purpose. Each one can choose what to do at any moment. There are no institutional top-down instructions. Instead contributing to the collective purpose and/or helping others to contribute is considered a good deed. All talks and ideas are welcome. Bottom-up structures discovered that "given enough eyeballs, all problems become shallow". In other words, given enough people putting their attention into something all problems become evident. Internet is a good example of bottom-up structure, we all bring some content to the internet (photos, comments, memes...) contributing to the purpose of having a rich and effective source of content for any purpose. Bottom-up structures include all cultural developments like arts and sciences (painting, music, physics, mathematics) in which each artist or scientist contributes a piece to the cultural domain he belongs to. As well, open-source software development are bottom up structures in which each programmer can read, copy, modify and propose improvements to the community using this software. Bottom-up structures have built-in the philosophy of "none of us is as smart as all of us".

I owe the idea of top-down and bottom-up structure to the programmer Eric Raymond Steven and to the economist Clay Shirky.

In 1453 the printing was invented. Before that date most people neither knew how to read nor to write. Most books had been written by religions. The printing supposed a cultural revival. Appart from literature, people started writing and reading on mathematics, physics, science, economics, medicine and a handful of other domains. Each of these subjects finally had a way to communicate through time and space, like religions did before, allowing them to accumulate a collective memory. The faster the communication the better bottom-up structures can collaborate. As Clay Shirky states it "communication is one of the big inputs to coordination". Indeed communication was the most expensive cost for culture to be broadcasted. Books supported this broadcasting during the Renaissance. Such a rich communication made us prosper in every field. And every field was developed bottom-up.

Books became the space for a collective memory.Internet has boosted memory spreading and communication of collective thought. As well, Internet is becoming the space for collective registers and accounts operations.

Revenue, income and profits for the stock holders. These include any software you pay for including online applications. Open source is much more didactic. One gets to learn to program by reading code, modifying it, running it and finding the most elegant solution, which is hacking. The world's best programmers use open source technologies because they enjoy learning from others' ideas so they have an interest in creating, contributing and sharing open source projects. The Great Monetary Reset

Blockchain is an open-source protocol that allows decentralized peer-to-peer transactions using cryptography. Let's tackle what "decentralized", "peer-to-peer" and "cryptography" mean. Open-source software is explained in the previous 2 paragraphs.

Decentralized means the servers are everywhere around the world. Anybody "Mining" is the name given to the work these servers do. This is because the one of these servers that resolves the problem first receives a fee.

"Peer-to-peer" means transactions do not happen through an intermediary. For example, currently bank intermediaries are big banks and the central banks. These also use centralized servers. Banks can be and have been hacked more than once. But the news media is afraid of loosing easy money from banks if they issue that kind of news.

Decentralization describes peer-to-peer networks and more secure than centralization. Centralization creates an information assymetry between individuals. People at the central institution that runs the system have a clear privilege. The central institution attract criminals, like cybercriminals. Decentralization incentivizes all people to participate in the system, it is an inclusive. Given enough eyeballs all security problems become shallow too. When so many programmers have money at stake in the blockchain, you can be peaceful that the software will perform securely. Here is a mathematical article on how game theory analyzes and ensures blockchain security. Decentralized public blockchains are more secure than banks' servers.

Open-source is more transparent than commercial software. Indeed all transactions performed on a blockchain are registered and verifiable. No currency pops out or disapears like with the banking tricks. When one wants to get a loan, another must lend his savings. Any person can read the code, execute it and check all is performed as it should. Any person can request comments, propose improvements to the blockchain and if it is interesting the idea will be implemented.

So blockchain is an alternative to the banking system. Since the birth of Bitcoin, other blockchains start ups have been financed with Bitcoins, like Ethereum in 2015. The use of blockchain as a alternative currency to fiat has been coined Blockchain 1.0 in the book Blockchain: Blueprint For A New Economy by Melanie Swan.

One step ahead of currency are smart contracts. Accordingly smart contracts have been coined blockchain 2.0. Bitcoin (2009) was originally just a currency and recently started supporting smart contracts. Ethereum (2014), the second largest blockchain by capitalization, was presented to the world as a decentralized applications platform. Smart contracts are obtained by using a decentralized blockchain and a shared memory, resulting in DApps: Decentralized Applications. Videos, comments and any file can stored in a shared memory and referenced by a transaction registered on the blockchain.

Smart contract platforms fulfill the need of central middle point server with an army of connected computers distributed around the globe, executing any imaginable application at a low cost. Market places like Amazon, Taobao and Alibaba that take a commission. Content sharing platforms like Twitter, Facebook, Reddit, Instagram and Linkedin. All video platforms. *Any* application can be claimed registered as a smart contract.

The next step is to integrate all these applications through a unique identity portal. Identity integration has been called Blockchain 3.0. It has some advantages and disadvantages. Advantages are to be ability to vote and travel identifying yourself with a virtual identity. Disadvantages are privacy, blockchain must increase freedom, not restrain it. Privacy, known as zero-knowledge transactions in the blockchain world, has also been a concern for blockchain communities since the beginning.
